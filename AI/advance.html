Current AI Technologies

In the world of AI, there are 2 types that exist: Reactive Machines and Limited Memory.

Reactive Machines is how AI perceives the world around it. It can carry out specific tasks but cannot be influenced to decide differently based on previous experiences. An example of this could be IBM's Deep Blue, which was used to win a game against a chess grandmaster back in 1997.

Limited Memory allows AI to perform tasks based upon previous experiences, unlike reactive machines. An example of this could be a Tesla on auto-pilot; a self-driving car.
